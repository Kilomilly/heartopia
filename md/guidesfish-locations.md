Fish Locations in Heartopia

Knowing where to fish is essential if you want to collect specific fish in Heartopia. Different bodies of water support different species, and many fish can only be found in certain locations or under specific conditions.

This guide provides a complete overview of all fish locations in Heartopia, explaining where to find fish, how locations differ, and where rare fish are most likely to appear.

For a full overview of fishing mechanics, see the Fishing Guide in Heartopia【internal link to /guides/fishing】.

Overview of Fishing Locations

Heartopia divides fishing areas into several natural categories. Each category has its own environment, fish pool, and progression role.

The main fishing locations include:

Lakes and ponds

Rivers and streams

Coastal waters

Special and hidden fishing spots

Understanding these locations will help you find the fish you need more efficiently.

Lakes and Ponds

Lakes and ponds are usually the first fishing locations players encounter in Heartopia. These calm bodies of water are easy to access and ideal for beginners.

What You Can Find in Lakes

Lakes mainly contain common freshwater fish. These fish are often used for early cooking recipes, crafting, and introductory quests.

Typical lake fish include:

Common Carp

Pond Minnow

Silver Carp

Night Catfish (appears at night)

Lakes are also a good place to practice fishing without pressure, as fish here usually have simple requirements.

Rivers and Streams

Rivers and streams add more variety to fishing in Heartopia. Because rivers flow through multiple regions, they often contain a wider range of fish.

River Fishing Characteristics

Greater fish variety than lakes

Some fish only appear in rivers

Certain river sections unlock later in the game

Common River Fish

River Bass

Silver Minnow

Bluefin Bass

Moonlight Eel (night only)

If you are searching for mid-game fish or recipe ingredients, rivers are often the best choice.

Coastal Waters

Coastal waters represent a major step forward in fishing progression. These locations are usually unlocked later and contain saltwater fish and higher-value species.

Why Coastal Fishing Matters

Access to rare and valuable fish

Ingredients for advanced cooking recipes

Required for certain quests and achievements

Fish Found in Coastal Waters

Coastal Snapper

Sea Bream

Tide Runner Fish

Deepwater Mackerel

Coastal fishing often rewards patience and exploration, as some areas may not be immediately visible on the map.

Special and Hidden Fishing Spots

Not all fish locations in Heartopia are clearly marked. Some fishing spots are hidden or unlocked only through exploration or story progression.

How to Find Hidden Fishing Spots

Explore less-traveled paths

Complete specific quests

Interact with certain characters

Revisit old areas after progressing

What Makes These Spots Unique

Hidden fishing spots often contain:

Rare fish

Location-exclusive species

Special quest-related fish

If you are searching for rare fish, these locations are worth the effort.

Rare Fish Locations in Heartopia

Rare fish usually appear under stricter conditions. They may require:

A specific location

A certain time of day

Game progression milestones

Where Rare Fish Are Most Common

Coastal waters

Mountain streams

Hidden fishing spots

Rare fish are often used for:

Special cooking recipes

High-level crafting

Quest objectives

For cooking-related uses, you can check recipes like Mushroom Pie【internal link to /recipes/mushroom-pie】.

Frequently Asked Questions
Where can I find fish in Heartopia?

Fish can be found in lakes, rivers, coastal waters, and special hidden locations throughout Heartopia.

Are fish location-specific?

Yes. Many fish only appear in certain locations, and some require specific conditions such as time of day.

Do rare fish have fixed locations?

Most rare fish appear in limited locations, often in coastal or hidden fishing spots.

Is fishing location more important than time?

Both matter. Location determines which fish can appear, while time of day affects availability.
