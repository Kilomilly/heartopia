Main Content
Heartopia Rainbow Bouquet Locations

During Rainbow weather in Heartopia, three special Rainbow Bouquet bubbles appear around the map. Popping these bubbles gives you Rainbow Bouquets, which can then be used to trigger a 30-minute Rainbow Blessing. Public guides currently agree on the basic system: there are always three bouquet bubbles per rainbow event, and collecting all three is one of the most important things to do before the weather window ends.

The main reason players search for this topic is simple: the event is limited, the flowers are valuable, and it is very easy to miss one if you do not know where to look first. Current guides also show that one bouquet is tied to your home area, while the remaining two appear at fixed event landmarks. However, public guides do not always describe the same landmark names in the same way, so the safest approach is to understand both the fixed logic and the most commonly reported route.

Quick Answer

If you only want the fastest answer, here is what matters most:

Rainbow Bouquets only appear during Rainbow weather

There are 3 bouquet bubbles each time

One bubble is always near your home plot

The other two appear at major fixed landmarks during the rainbow event

If one seems missing, check your mailbox and confirm the rainbow is still active

How to Know When Rainbow Bouquet Bubbles Are Available

Rainbow Bouquet bubbles do not appear all the time. They are tied to Rainbow weather, which must be checked through your in-game Watch. Current guides explain that the Watch forecast is the best way to plan ahead for the event. Some guides also note that rainbow events are limited and only last a few real-time hours once active.

That means the smartest routine is:

Check your Watch

Confirm Rainbow weather is active

Start collecting bouquet bubbles immediately

Visit Doris and do other Rainbow activities after

All Rainbow Bouquet Locations
Bouquet 1: Outside Your Home

This is the easiest bouquet to forget, even though it is usually the simplest one to collect.

Public guides consistently describe the first Rainbow Bouquet bubble as appearing outside your home or in front of your house. Because Heartopia places players into available home slots, the exact map position can differ depending on your current housing placement, but the rule stays the same: check your own home plot first. One guide also warns that if you do not see the bouquet there, you should check your in-game mailbox.

Best search tip:
Always collect the home bouquet first before running across town. It is the one most players miss because they assume all three are in public landmarks.

Bouquet 2: Major Town Landmark

The second bouquet is commonly reported at a major public landmark. One detailed guide places it floating in front of the Town Hall door, while another guide places the second bouquet in the Forest Island route area. That difference likely reflects either route naming differences, location interpretation, or guide-specific event-day mapping. What matters most is that this bouquet is not random in the same way as your home slot; it is tied to a major event landmark.

If you are using the most commonly surfaced public route, the first public landmark to check is:

Town Hall entrance area

If your event route or map reference follows the alternative guide structure, also be ready to check:

Forest Island central landmark / giant climbable rock area

Bouquet 3: Final Event Landmark

The third bouquet is also tied to a public landmark, but this is where public guides diverge again. One strong guide says it circles the Whale Statue, while another places the final bouquet at Forest Lake. Because these are both landmark-style event zones rather than home-linked locations, the practical takeaway is that your final bouquet will be at another clearly visible rainbow-event landmark rather than hidden in a random field.

If you are following the best-known route from current SERP results, check:

Whale Statue area

If you are following the alternative Rainbow event route, also check:

Forest Lake

What Rainbow Bouquets Do

Rainbow Bouquets are not just collectibles. They provide a Rainbow Blessing that lasts 30 minutes. Current guides describe this blessing as improving the quality of results across multiple hobbies, including fishing, cooking, catching insects, photographing birds, and snowman or sculpting activities. Screen Hype specifically says that when the blessing activates, the item you just collected gains an extra star. Phrasemaker also notes that watering plants during the blessing can give you a chance to trigger Rainbow Breeding Powder effects.

That makes Rainbow Bouquets one of the most useful parts of the rainbow event, especially if you plan to:

fish during rainbow weather

catch Rainbow-related insects

improve hobby output quality

combine the blessing with gardening progression

Best Order to Collect Rainbow Bouquets

The most efficient order is:

Your home plot

Final statue / lake landmark

This route works because it removes the most commonly forgotten bouquet first and leaves the fixed landmark checks for later. It also makes it easier to bundle bouquet collection with other rainbow activities such as visiting Doris or heading toward rainbow-weather fishing spots. Public player discussion reinforces that the home bouquet is easy to miss, which makes this order especially useful.

Why You Might Not Be Finding All 3

If you cannot find all three Rainbow Bouquet bubbles, check these first:

1. Rainbow weather may not actually be active
Bouquets only appear during Rainbow weather, so always confirm it through your Watch.

2. You forgot the home bouquet
This is the most common mistake. Many players search public landmarks first and forget the one tied to their home plot.

3. Your home placement may differ from another guide’s screenshots
Because players can load into different housing slots, “outside your home” will not look identical for every account.

4. Check your mailbox
One published guide specifically notes that if you do not see the bouquet near your home, you should check your in-game mailbox.

5. You may be following a guide that uses a different landmark naming convention
Some public guides identify Town Hall and Whale Statue, while others label Forest Island and Forest Lake. The best fix is to treat them as the major public landmark stops for your server’s event route rather than assuming every screenshot will match perfectly.

Rainbow Bouquets vs Doris

Players often search these together because both are tied to Rainbow weather.

Doris appears under or near the rainbow and sells Rainbow-specific items such as colored sugar, postcards, and a Rainbow emote.

Rainbow Bouquet bubbles are separate event objects that give you the Rainbow Blessing buff.

If you are planning your route well, collect the bouquets first, then go to Doris, then use your blessing while doing fishing, insects, or gardening.
