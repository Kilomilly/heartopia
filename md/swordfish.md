Swordfish in Heartopia

Swordfish is one of the most sought-after high-level fish in Heartopia because it is tied to Rainbow weather, requires Fishing Level 10, and is consistently grouped with the best ocean catches in public guides. It is also one of the fish most likely to confuse players, because different guides describe its location slightly differently — some label it under Whale Sea, while others place it under Sea Fishing or Deep Sea / boat access. What these guides agree on is that Swordfish is a top-tier rainbow ocean fish, not something you catch in rivers or standard beginner lakes.

That means the real challenge is not just the fish itself. The real challenge is knowing which ocean access route to use, whether Rainbow weather is truly active, and whether you are fishing at the correct time of day. That is exactly why so many players search this keyword directly instead of relying on broad fish lists.

Quick Answer

If you only want the shortest possible answer, here it is:

Fish: Swordfish

Fishing Level: 10

Weather: Rainbow

Best Time: commonly reported as daytime, especially 6 AM–6 PM

Area: high-level ocean access, usually described as Whale Sea or Sea Fishing / Boat access depending on the guide

If you are not in Rainbow weather, not level 10, or not using the correct sea route, Swordfish will not appear.

Where to Catch Swordfish

The public results do not describe Swordfish in exactly the same way, so the safest way to explain it is this:

Swordfish is consistently described as a high-level Rainbow ocean fish. Some guides place it in Whale Sea, while others describe it as part of Sea Fishing or a Deep Sea / Boat-access route tied to Bill in Fishing Village. These do not necessarily contradict each other in practical gameplay terms, because both point you toward advanced ocean fishing rather than regular coastal or inland fishing.

If you want the most reliable player-facing route, treat Swordfish as a fish you should chase through the Fishing Village → Bill → sea-fishing / advanced ocean access path first. That is the clearest route supported by current public event-style guides.

Swordfish Requirements

To catch Swordfish in Heartopia, current public guides most commonly agree on these requirements:

Fishing Level 10

Rainbow weather

Ocean / sea-fishing access

Daytime window, commonly listed as 6 AM–6 PM or described more generally as morning / afternoon

The Fishing Level 10 requirement is especially important. If you are below that level, you can waste an entire Rainbow window chasing the wrong target.

How to Reach the Right Swordfish Area

The most practical route is:

Fast travel to Fishing Village

Find Bill near the boats

Start the Sea Fishing or boat-access route

Fish in the advanced ocean area during Rainbow weather

Prioritize large ocean shadows and keep reeling discipline tight

Heartopia.Life’s Sea Fishing event guide explains that Bill is the key NPC for getting into advanced sea fishing access, and that the event includes exclusive fish and tension-based mechanics. That makes this the best user-facing explanation for players who are unsure where to begin.

Is Swordfish Rainbow-Only?

Public guides strongly associate Swordfish with Rainbow weather, and several fish-location summaries explicitly list Swordfish under rainbow conditions. Heartopia.Life also groups Swordfish in its Rainbow Weather Fish (High Value) section and specifically tells players to prioritize sea fishing when rainbow appears.

That means the best practical rule is:

If the weather is not Rainbow, do not plan a Swordfish trip.

When to Catch Swordfish

Most current public references describe Swordfish as a daytime fish, usually in the 6 AM to 6 PM range or in broader “morning and afternoon” language. That level of consistency is good enough to treat daytime as the safest active window.

So if Rainbow weather appears at night, it may still be worth using the event for other fish, but Swordfish should not be your first expectation unless your own in-game collection confirms a different condition.

Why Swordfish Is Worth Catching

Swordfish is consistently treated as one of the best Rainbow priority catches in current public guides. It appears alongside Bluefin Tuna and other high-tier ocean fish in “best money” and “rare fish” sections, which means it is worth prioritizing over lower-value lake or river species during limited Rainbow windows.

In practical terms, Swordfish matters for three reasons:

it is a late-game prestige catch

it is part of the valuable Rainbow fish pool

it is a better use of rare Rainbow time than common or low-value fish

Why You Might Not Be Finding Swordfish

If Swordfish is not spawning for you, check these in order:

1. Your Fishing Level may be too low
Swordfish is repeatedly listed at Level 10.

2. It may not actually be Rainbow weather
Several guides tell players to check their Watch and only chase Rainbow fish when the event is active.

3. You may be in the wrong ocean route
This is the most common problem. Swordfish is not treated like a basic shoreline fish. Use the advanced sea-fishing / boat route first.

4. You may be fishing at the wrong time
Most current sources point to daytime. If you are testing night spawns, Swordfish is a poor target.

5. You may be chasing the wrong shadow type
Advanced sea fish are generally tied to larger silhouettes, and the sea-fishing guide also explains line tension and rare shadow logic.

Best Strategy During a Rainbow Event

If Rainbow weather begins and you want Swordfish, this is the best order of action:

Confirm Rainbow in your Watch

Travel immediately to Fishing Village

Use Bill’s sea-fishing access

Spend your first part of the event on high-value ocean targets such as Swordfish and Bluefin Tuna

Only move to other rainbow fish if ocean access is not available or after securing your main catches
