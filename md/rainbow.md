Heartopia Rainbow Fish Guide

Rainbow weather is one of the best fishing opportunities in Heartopia. It gives access to rare fish, higher-value catches, and some of the most important collection targets in the game. The problem is that rainbow events are limited, so players often waste time deciding where to go first or which fish are actually worth prioritizing.

This guide solves that problem. It explains which fish matter most during rainbow weather, where to catch them, how to use your limited time efficiently, and what to check if you cannot find the fish you want.

Quick Answer

If you only want the short version, here is the key information:

Rainbow fish are fish that either only appear during Rainbow weather or become much easier to catch during Rainbow weather.

The most important rainbow-only targets are high-value species such as Swordfish, Golden King Crab, Bluefin Tuna, Smooth Hammerhead, Butterfly Koi, and Huchen.

The best short route is:

Deep Sea first

Giantwood River second

Lakes last

If your goal is pure profit, focus on rainbow-exclusive fish first. If your goal is collection progress, catch at least one of each exclusive species before farming duplicates.

Rainbow Fish Types in Heartopia

The most useful way to understand rainbow fish is to split them into two groups.

1. Rainbow-Exclusive Fish

These fish are the highest priority because they are tied directly to rainbow weather. If you miss the weather window, you miss the chance to catch them. This is one of the strongest ideas in your attachment, and it is exactly the kind of thing many competitors fail to explain clearly.

Fish	Location	Time	Fishing Level	Value	Priority
Butterfly Koi	Sea (Bill’s Boat)	All Day	8	550g	Very High
Golden King Crab	Sea (Bill’s Boat)	All Day	9	600g	Very High
Swordfish	Deep Sea	All Day	10	600g	Very High
Bluefin Tuna	Deep Sea	Dawn / Dusk	10	580g	Very High
Smooth Hammerhead	Deep Sea	All Day	10	535g	High
Huchen	Giantwood River	All Day	9	460g	High

These are your first targets when rainbow weather begins. They have the highest value, the highest urgency, and the strongest collection importance.

2. Rainbow-Enhanced Fish

These fish can appear in other conditions too, but rainbow weather makes them more worthwhile to farm. They are useful when you have already caught the exclusive fish you need, or when your fishing level is not high enough for the top-tier rainbow-only targets.

Fish	Location	Time	Fishing Level	Value	Also Spawns In

These are not the fish you should rush first if rainbow time is limited, but they are very useful for collection and efficient leveling.

Best 2-Hour Rainbow Fishing Route

One of the best ideas in your attachment is the time-boxed rainbow route. Competitors often list fish, but they do not tell users what to do first, second, and third. That is a real opportunity gap.

Phase 1: Deep Sea Priority

Start at Fishing Village, rent Bill’s boat, and go to Deep Sea first. This is where the highest-value catches are concentrated. Your top targets here are:

Swordfish

Bluefin Tuna

Smooth Hammerhead

Golden King Crab

Butterfly Koi

Deep Sea should be your first stop because it contains the highest-value rainbow-exclusive fish, and these are the most important catches to secure before the weather window ends.

Phase 2: Giantwood River

After Deep Sea, move to Giantwood River.

Your main target is Huchen, with Zander as a useful secondary catch. This phase is efficient because the catches are faster than Deep Sea, and the fish still have strong value.

Phase 3: Lake Sweep

Finish with Meadow Lake, Forest Lake, or Onsen Mountain Lake, depending on your level and goals.

Use this phase for:

Goldfish

Large Pearl Mussel

Tadpole

This is the lowest-priority phase for advanced players, but it is still a good use of leftover rainbow time if you want collection progress or easier leveling.

How to Tell If Rainbow Weather Is Active

Another strong point in the attachment is the reminder that players need a very clear answer for weather confirmation. Competitors often mention weather, but they do not make it actionable enough.

To confirm rainbow weather:

Open your smartwatch

Check the weather icon

Look for the rainbow symbol

Confirm the event is active before traveling to fishing spots

Best Strategy by Player Type

This is another excellent section from the attachment and worth keeping in a shorter, cleaner form. It makes the page useful to more users without turning it into a wall of text.

For New Players (Low Fishing Level)

If you are still early in the game, do not stress about the highest-value rainbow-exclusive fish yet. You may not have the level or tools for them. Instead:

fish in easier lake or river zones

catch everything you can

use rainbow weather for fast XP

prepare for higher-level events later

For Mid-Game Players

If you are in the middle range, focus on:

Goldfish

Zander

Large Pearl Mussel

These give you a mix of profit, progression, and realistic difficulty.

For Advanced Players

If you are high level, your priorities should be:

Deep Sea first

rainbow-exclusive fish first

sell raw fish during the event

avoid wasting time on low-value catches

This is where the route in the earlier section matters most.

For Completionists

If your goal is journal completion, catch one of every rainbow-exclusive species first, then use the rest of the event to fill in enhanced fish or duplicates.

Troubleshooting: Why You May Not Be Finding Rainbow Fish

This section is one of the biggest competitive advantages identified in the competitor analysis. Most ranking pages do not do enough troubleshooting.

Check 1: Is It Really Rainbow Weather?

Do not assume. Confirm it through the watch or visible in-game indicators.

Check 2: Are You in the Right Location?

Some players waste time in the wrong zone.

Swordfish: Deep Sea

Huchen: Giantwood River

Goldfish: Meadow Lake

A correct weather condition does not help if the location is wrong.

Check 3: Is the Time of Day Correct?

Not every fish is all-day.

For example:

Bluefin Tuna has a Dawn / Dusk requirement

Goldfish has Morning / Day / Evening timing

Time filters matter.

Check 4: Is Your Fishing Level High Enough?

Some rainbow fish are simply level-locked.

If you are below the requirement, they will remain out of reach no matter how good the weather is.

Check 5: Are You Losing Fish During the Fight?

If fish keep escaping, slow down and manage tension instead of over-reeling. Your attachment is right to emphasize rhythm and patience here.

Best Practices During Rainbow Weather

To get the most out of rainbow time:

clear inventory before the event

bring bait and utility items

prioritize exclusive fish first

avoid cooking during the event window

sell and reset only when necessary

The original draft’s “sell raw, cook later” logic is especially strong and worth keeping. It matches the event’s time pressure and improves real efficiency.

FAQ
What are Rainbow Fish in Heartopia?

They are fish tied to rainbow weather, either as exclusive spawns or as much better farming opportunities during rainbow events.

Which rainbow fish should I catch first?

Start with the rainbow-exclusive fish, especially the Deep Sea targets and Huchen. Those are the highest urgency catches.

What is the best rainbow fishing route?

Deep Sea first, Giantwood River second, and Lakes last. This is the clearest route advantage identified in your draft.

Why can’t I find Swordfish or Bluefin Tuna?

Most likely because one of these is wrong:

weather

location

time of day

fishing level

Are all rainbow fish equally important?

No. Your competitor analysis correctly points out that many ranking pages fail to separate must-catch fish from nice-to-have fish. Exclusive fish should come first.
